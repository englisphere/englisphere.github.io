Past Tenses in English

1. Learning Material

In English, there are several forms of past tenses. Each one gives a different nuance about the timing, completion, and connection of past events.

Types of Past Tenses

TenseStructureUsageExample
Past SimpleSubject + V2Completed actions at a specific time in the past."She visited Paris last year."
Past ContinuousSubject + was/were + V-ingOngoing action in the past, often interrupted."They were watching TV when I called."
Past PerfectSubject + had + V3Action completed before another past action."She had finished dinner before he arrived."
Past Perfect ContinuousSubject + had been + V-ingOngoing action that was happening before another action or time in the past."He had been studying for three hours when the phone rang."

Key Points

  • Past Simple = finished actions ("I walked to school.")
  • Past Continuous = background action ("I was walking when it started to rain.")
  • Past Perfect = action completed before another ("I had walked before it rained.")
  • Past Perfect Continuous = ongoing action that had duration ("I had been walking for hours.")

Signal Words

Certain words often hint at which tense you should use:

Signal WordLikely Tense
Yesterday, Last week, In 1990Past Simple
While, When, AsPast Continuous
Before, After, Already, By the timePast Perfect
For, Since, Before (with duration)Past Perfect Continuous

2. Practice Exercises

A. Multiple Choice

Choose the correct tense for each sentence:

  1. When I arrived, they _________ dinner.
    a) had finished  b) were finishing  c) have finished
  2. I _________ for two hours before the guests finally showed up.
    a) waited  b) was waiting  c) had been waiting
  3. While he _________ the report, his boss called him.
    a) had written  b) was writing  c) wrote
  4. By the time we got to the cinema, the movie _________.
    a) started  b) had started  c) was starting

B. Fill in the Blanks

Use the correct past tense form of the verbs in parentheses:

  1. When I (arrive) __________ at the station, the train (already/leave) __________.
  2. She (write) __________ emails all morning before she finally (take) __________ a break.
  3. They (not/finish) __________ the project when the manager (ask) __________ for updates.
  4. While we (walk) __________ through the forest, it (start) __________ to rain heavily.

C. Short Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) describing a day when something unexpected happened. Use all four past tenses at least once!

Example starter: "Last Sunday, I woke up earlier than usual. While I was making coffee, I heard a loud noise outside..."

3. Extra Challenge: Spot the Mistakes!

Find and correct the mistakes in these sentences:

  • She had went to the market before it was opened.
  • I was finish my homework when you called.
  • They had been played football for two hours when it started to rain.
  • While he wrote the letter, he had thinking about his future.
