Conversation: Asking for & Giving Directions

Knowing how to ask for directions is an important everyday skill, especially when you’re in a new place or traveling. In this guide, you’ll learn how to:

  • Politely ask for directions
  • Understand common responses
  • Use key vocabulary and phrases with confidence

Let’s help you avoid getting lost and start asking like a pro!

 

Asking for Directions Politely

This section teaches you how to start a conversation in a respectful and natural way. Being polite helps people feel more comfortable and willing to help you.

Phrase

Explanation & Example

Excuse me, can you help me?

Use this to get someone’s attention nicely.

“Excuse me, can you help me? I’m looking for the library.”

How do I get to…?

Use this when you want directions to a place.

“How do I get to the park?”

Can you tell me the way to…?

A polite way to ask where something is.

“Can you tell me the way to the nearest bank?”

Where is the…?

A short and direct question.

“Where is the hospital?”

Is it far from here?

Ask this if you want to know how close the place is.

“Is the train station far from here?”

Part 2: Giving Directions

In this part, you’ll learn how to understand or give directions clearly, using everyday phrases. These are the most common phrases someone might say when explaining how to get somewhere.

Common Vocabulary

These words are often used in directions. Knowing them helps you understand instructions better and also helps you give clearer directions to others.

Use

traffic lights

A circular road junction

Sample Conversation

Anna (Tourist): Excuse me, can you help me? I’m trying to find the city library.

Toni (Native)  : Sure! It’s not far from here.

Anna               : Great! How do I get there?

Toni                 : Go straight down this street for about two blocks. Then, turn left at the traffic lights.

Anna               : Okay, two blocks and then left at the lights. Got it.

Toni                 : After you turn left, you’ll see a big park. The library is right next to it, across from the coffee shop.

Anna               : Is it walking distance?

Toni                 : Yes, it should only take about 10 minutes.

Anna               : Thank you so much for your help!

Toni                 : You’re welcome! Enjoy your visit.

 

Quick Tips

  • Speak clearly and slowly
  • Use your hands or draw a map if needed
  • Repeat the directions back to check understanding
  • Use GPS or maps as backup!

Map-Based Practice

  • Look at the map provided. It shows several places (e.g., a bank, school, library, supermarket, etc.).
  • Work in pairs or small groups.
  • One student asks a question such as:
    “How do I get to the bank from the school?”
  • The other student answers by giving clear directions using full sentences and proper direction phrases (e.g., "Go straight," "Turn left," "It's next to...").
  • Then, switch roles and try with different places on the map.
