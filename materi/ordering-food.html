ORDERING FOOD AT A RESTAURANT: A SIMPLE GUIDE

Have you ever felt nervous or confused when ordering food in English? Maybe you’ve been to an international restaurant, a trendy café, or watched a scene in a movie where someone orders food fluently and confidently. In this section, you’ll learn how to order food in English politely, clearly, and with confidence. You’ll also explore the differences between Western-style and Indonesian-style menus, along with key vocabulary and expressions commonly used in restaurant settings. Ready to sound like a pro when ordering your meal in English? Let’s get started!

The Differentiation of Menus at Indonesian and Western Restaurant

In Indonesia, menus are usually not separated into specific categories like appetizers, main courses, or desserts. This is different from western restaurants, where dishes are typically arranged in a full-course set from starters to desserts.

Appetizer

Western Style: Small dishes served before the main course to stimulate your appetite.
Examples: Garlic bread, soup, salad, mozzarella sticks.

Indonesian Style: Not commonly served as a separate course. Sometimes snacks like risoles, pastel, or tahu isi can be considered as appetizers, but they’re often eaten any time, not just before the main meal.

Main Course

Western Style: The central and most filling part of the meal. Examples: Grilled chicken with mashed potatoes, steak, pasta, burgers.

Indonesian Style: Usually consists of nasi (rice) + side dishes like ayam goreng, tempe orek, sambal, sayur asem. In Indonesia, rice-based dishes are often the focus of any meal and usually not separated into "courses".

Dessert

Western Style: Sweet treats eaten after the main course. Examples: Ice cream, cake, pudding, brownies.

Indonesian Style: Desserts do exist, but are often enjoyed separately or at different times. Examples: Es campur, kolak, klepon, dadar gulung.

Beverage/Drink

Both Indonesian and Western restaurants serve a variety of drinks.

Western: soda, juice, milkshakes, coffee, tea, wine (in some).

Indonesian: es teh manis, es jeruk, wedang jahe, kopi tubruk.

Commonly Used Expressions by Customer

  • “Can I see the menu, please?”
  • “Excuse me, could we have the menu, please?”
  • “I’d like to order ___.”
  • “What do you recommend?”
  • “What’s the most popular dish?”
  • “I’m allergic to ___.”
  • “Can I have the bill, please?”
  • “Can we get a table for four?”
  • “I’d like it to go / for takeaway.”
  • “Excuse me, I need a fork/spoon.”
  • “Is there a table available?”
  • “Can I change my order?”
  • “I’d like the same as that, please.”
  • “Could I have some more water, please?”
  • “Can we get separate bills?”

Commonly Used Expressions by Waiter/Waitress

  • “Welcome to [restaurant name], may I help you?”
  • “Are you ready to order?”
  • “Would you like something to drink?”
  • “Today’s special is ___.”
  • “How would you like your steak?”
  • “Would you like anything else?”
  • “Let me know if you need anything else.”
  • “Is everything alright?”
  • “The food will be ready in about 10 minutes.”
  • “Here is your bill.”
  • “Do you have a reservation?”
  • “Please wait to be seated.”
  • “We’re out of that item at the moment.”
  • “Thank you for dining with us!”
  • “Have a great meal!”
  • “Thank you, come again!”

Example of Dialogue at a Restaurant

Waiter  : Good evening. Welcome to Ocean Breeze Restaurant. Do you have a reservation?
Lisa    : Good evening. No, we don’t. Is there a table available?
Waiter  : Let me check... We’re quite full right now, but I can put your name on the waiting list. It will take around 15 minutes.
Lisa    : That’s fine. We’ll wait.
Waiter  : Great. May I have your name, please?
Lisa    : Lisa.
Waiter  : Thank you, Lisa. I’ll call you when your table is ready.

(After 15 minutes)
Waiter  : Lisa, your table is ready. Please follow me.
Lisa    : Thank you!

Waiter  : Here’s the menu. I’ll be back shortly to take your order.
Lisa    : Thank you.

(A few minutes later)
Waiter  : Are you ready to order?
Lisa    : Yes. I’ll have the grilled chicken with mashed potatoes, please. And a glass of iced tea.
Waiter  : Great choice. Would you like any dessert later?
Lisa    : I’ll decide after the meal.
Waiter  : No problem. I’ll be right back with your drink.
