🗣 ECC English Vibes: Idioms & Slang Expressions

What are Idioms and Slang?

Idioms: Phrases that have a meaning different from the literal words.

📌 Example: “It’s raining cats and dogs.” (It means: It’s raining heavily, not literally cats and dogs!)

Slang: Informal expressions often used in casual conversation.

📌 Example: “That movie was lit!” (It means: The movie was amazing or exciting.)

🔥 Popular Idioms to Know

IdiomMeaningExample Sentence
Break a legGood luck“Break a leg in your test tomorrow!”
Under the weatherFeeling sick“I’m a bit under the weather today.”
Piece of cakeVery easy“This assignment is a piece of cake.”
Hit the booksStudy hard“I need to hit the books tonight.”
Let the cat out of the bagReveal a secret“He let the cat out of the bag too early.”

💬 Common Slang Expressions

SlangMeaningExample Sentence
LitAwesome, cool“That concert was lit!”
ChillRelax / Relaxed person“Let’s just chill this weekend.”
BFFBest Friend Forever“She’s my BFF since elementary school.”
No capSeriously / I’m not lying“That was the best food ever, no cap.”
Ghost (v)Disappear suddenly (in chat/talk)“He ghosted me after one text.”

📌 Tips to Learn Idioms & Slang

  • 🎧 Listen to English songs, movies, or vlogs – Pay attention to casual phrases!
  • 📖 Keep an “Idiom & Slang Journal” – Write new ones you learn and their meanings.
  • 💬 Use them in conversations – Try to use at least 1 new expression a day!
