📘 ECC Grammar Corner: Simple Present Tense

✅ What is the Simple Present Tense?

Simple Present Tense is used to describe:

  • Daily routines 🛏🚌📚
  • General truths 🌍☀
  • Fixed schedules 🕒🗓
  • Facts ✅

🔑 Formula

1. (+) Positive Sentences

Subject + Verb 1 (s/es) + Object

SubjectExample
I/You/We/TheyI eat breakfast at 6 AM.
He/She/ItShe eats breakfast at 6 AM.

📝 Add s/es only for He/She/It!

2. (-) Negative Sentences

Subject + do/does + not + Verb 1

SubjectExample
I/You/We/TheyThey do not (don’t) play football.
He/She/ItHe does not (doesn’t) play football.

3. (?) Interrogative Sentences

Do/Does + Subject + Verb 1 + ?

QuestionAnswer
Do you like English?Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.
Does she speak Japanese?Yes, she does. / No, she doesn’t.

Quick Rules

  • He/She/It → Verb + s/es
    Example: She watches TV.
  • Don’t add “s” if using does.
    Example: ✅ She does not watch TV. ❌ She does not watches TV.

ECC Grammar Practice

📝 A. Fill in the blanks

  • My brother ___ (go) to school every day.
  • They ___ (not like) spicy food.
  • ___ she ___ (study) English?
  • I always ___ (drink) tea in the morning.
  • He ___ (not watch) movies at night.

🗣 B. Make Sentences

Use these verbs to make sentences in Simple Present:

play – eat – live – read – walk

Example: 🖊 “I read books every night.”

🎯 ECC Speaking Prompt (Simple Present Edition)

Use the Simple Present to answer:

  • What time do you wake up?
  • What do you do after school?
  • Do you help your parents at home?
  • What food do you like?
  • Does your friend play a musical instrument?

🔄 Practice with a partner: Ask and answer using the structure!
