ECC Journaling Activity: Express Yourself in English!

🌟 What is Journaling?

Journaling is the habit of writing down your thoughts, feelings, experiences, or reflections in a personal space — like a diary, but in English! It's a great way to improve your English fluency, vocabulary, and self-expression.

Why Should You Journal?

  • Practice Writing Skills: Regular journaling helps you build grammar and sentence structure naturally.
  • Express Your Thoughts: It’s your safe space to be honest and creative.
  • Track Your Growth: Looking back at old entries shows your progress in English!

📝 How to Start Journaling

You can follow this simple format to get started:

  1. Date & Title
    Example: 📅 April 19, 2025
    🖊 A Rainy Day at School
  2. Opening Sentence (Mood Check)
    Start with how you feel.
    Example: “Today I felt a bit tired because I slept late last night.”
  3. Main Body (Experience or Reflection)
    Tell a short story or describe your day.
    Example: “We had a school event today. I joined the singing competition, and even though I didn’t win, I had so much fun.”
  4. Closing (Lesson or Hope)
    End with what you learned or what you hope for.
    Example: “I learned that confidence is more important than winning. I hope to join again next year!”

📝 Journal Prompts (Latihan Soal)

  • Describe your favorite place and why you love it.
  • Write about a person who inspires you.
  • What did you learn this week at school?
  • Write about a challenge you faced and how you handled it.
  • Imagine your future self in 10 years — what are you doing?

Tip: You only need to write 5–10 sentences per journal entry. Keep it simple and honest!

🛠 Journaling Tools and Ideas

  • Use Your Phone Notes or a Journal Book
  • Write at the Same Time Everyday (e.g. before bed)
  • Add Drawings, Stickers, or Photos to Your Journal
  • Use English Emojis or Hashtags to Make it Fun! Example: Feeling great today! #SunshineMood ☀
