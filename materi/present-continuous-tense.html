GRAMMAR’S TIME: PRESENT CONTINUOUS TENSE

Hi, everyone! Have you ever wanted to tell someone what you’re doing right now? Like “I’m eating” or “She is studying”? To talk about actions happening at the moment, we use the Present Continuous Tense. Let’s learn it together!

Definition

The Present Continuous Tense is used to describe actions that are happening right now or around the current time.

Formula:

(+) Subject + to be (am/is/are) + Verb-ing

(-) Subject + not + to be (am/is/are) + Verb-ing

(?) To be (am/is/are) + Subject + Verb-ing

(+) Subject + to be (am/is/are) + Verb-ing

(-) Subject + not + to be (am/is/are) + Verb-ing

(?) To be (am/is/are) + Subject + Verb-ing

  • Use is with singular subjects like he, she, it
  • Use are with plural subjects like they, we, and also with you
  • Use am only with I

Examples:

  • (+) I am working on my homework.
  • (-) I am not working on my homework.
  • (?) Am I working on my homework?

  • (+) They are studying for the test.
  • (-) They are not studying for the test.
  • (?) Are they studying for the test?

  • (+) She is sleeping right now.
  • (-) She is not sleeping right now.
  • (?) Is she sleeping right now?

Usage

  • To talk about actions happening right now
  • To describe temporary activities
  • To express something happening around now (but not necessarily this exact moment)
  • To describe changing or developing situations

Examples of the Conversation Text

Text 1

Gita : “Hi, Ayu! What are you doing right now?”
Ayu  : “Hey, Gita! I’m watching a movie with my sister. What about you?”
Gita : “I’m doing my homework. It’s a bit difficult, so I needed a break.”
Ayu  : “I see. Are you working on the English assignment?”
Gita : “Yes, I am. Are you planning to finish it today?”
Ayu  : “Not yet. I’m still thinking about the topic. Maybe I’ll start after lunch.”
Gita : “Alright, let me know if you need help. I’m reading the textbook now to find some ideas.”
Ayu  : “Thanks, Gita! I really appreciate it.”

Text 2

Raka : "Hi, Sinta! Are you going to the canteen now?"
Sinta: "Not yet. I’m waiting for Lani. She’s talking to the teacher."
Raka : "Oh, I see. I’m feeling so hungry right now."
Sinta: "Me too! What are you planning to buy?"
Raka : "I’m thinking about getting fried rice. What about you?"
Sinta: "I’m not eating much today. I’m just buying a snack and tea."
Raka : "Alright. Let’s go together after Lani comes."
Sinta: "Sure! She’s walking this way now."

Quiz Time

Instructions: Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb in Present Continuous Tense. Use am, is, or are + verb + -ing.

  1. I __________ (do) my homework right now.
  2. She __________ (talk) to her friend on the phone.
  3. They __________ (watch) a movie in the living room.
  4. We __________ (study) for the English test.
  5. My brother __________ (play) football in the yard.
  6. You __________ (write) so fast!
  7. The teacher __________ (explain) the lesson clearly.
  8. I __________ (not listen) to music at the moment.
  9. What __________ you __________ (do) now?
  10. Look! The children __________ (run) in the park.
