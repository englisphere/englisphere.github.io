Watching and Reviewing a Short Movie

Purpose of the Lesson

  • To understand and enjoy audiovisual texts (short films).
  • To express opinions and write a simple movie review using appropriate language.

1. Before Watching

Pre-watching activity:

  • Activate background knowledge:
    • What kind of movies do you like?
    • Have you ever watched a short film?
  • Learn key vocabulary related to the film (example: characters, plot, conflict, setting).

2. While Watching

Focus on the following elements:

  • Characters – Who are the people in the story?
  • Setting – Where and when does the story happen?
  • Plot – What is the story about? (Beginning – Middle – End)
  • Conflict – What problem happens in the story?
  • Message/Moral – What can we learn from the story?

Use a viewing guide or worksheet:

ElementNotes
Title
Main Characters
Setting
Problem
Solution/Ending
Message
Your Opinion

3. After Watching

Write or present a short review. Use this structure:

Structure of a Short Movie Review:

  1. Title and Introduction
    • What is the title of the movie?
    • Who are the main characters?
    • What is the genre?
  2. Summary
    • Briefly describe the story (no spoilers if possible).
  3. Opinion
    • Did you like it? Why or why not?
    • Which part was your favorite?
    • What can be improved?
  4. Recommendation
    • Would you recommend this movie to others?

Example of a Short Review

Title: Piper (Pixar)

Piper is a short animated film about a baby sandpiper bird learning how to get food on the beach. The animation is beautiful, and the story is simple but touching. I love how the little bird becomes brave. I recommend this film because it’s inspiring and suitable for all ages. ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ (4 out of 5 stars)

Useful Expressions:

  • The story is about...
  • The animation is...
  • My favorite scene is...
  • I think the message is...
  • I would recommend this movie because...
